Our Mission

The Hayti Heritage Center is committed to preserving and celebrating the cultural, historical, and artistic legacy of the Hayti community. We aim to serve as a beacon of cultural enrichment, providing educational programs, art exhibits, and community events that highlight the contributions of African Americans to society.

History of the Center

Established in the late 19th century, the Hayti Heritage Center is a cornerstone of African American culture in Durham, North Carolina. The center is housed in the historic St. Joseph's AME Church, a landmark that has served as a cultural hub for the Hayti community for over a century. Through its programs and initiatives, the center strives to honor and preserve the rich history of the area.

Programs and Services

The Hayti Heritage Center offers a variety of programs and services including:

  • Art Exhibits: Showcasing works from local and national African American artists.
  • Educational Workshops: Providing learning opportunities on African American history and culture.
  • Community Events: Hosting festivals, concerts, and other community gatherings.
  • Historical Tours: Offering guided tours of the historic Hayti district and St. Joseph's AME Church.

Get Involved

We invite you to get involved with the Hayti Heritage Center by volunteering, attending our events, or making a donation to support our mission. Together, we can continue to celebrate and preserve the rich cultural heritage of the Hayti community.
